# Collection Criteria

This file defines what "collect" vs "skip" means. Edit it freely — every
run reads this file fresh, so changes take effect immediately with no code
changes needed.

Works the same regardless of link type (article, YouTube video, or social
post) — write your rules in terms of *topic and content*, not source type,
and the judge will apply them consistently across all three.

The example below monitors AI/tech product news. Replace it with your own
rules for whatever you're tracking (a competitor, an industry, a person, a
stock, etc.).

## ✅ Collect

- Articles, videos, or posts announcing a new AI product, model, or major
  feature launch
- Funding rounds, acquisitions, or partnerships involving AI companies
- Content reporting a measurable real-world impact of an AI product
  (adoption numbers, benchmark results, notable customer wins)
- Critical or investigative coverage (safety concerns, controversies,
  regulatory scrutiny) about an AI company or product
- The same story covered by multiple sources — collect each one
  separately, even if the content overlaps

## ❌ Skip

- Pure press releases with no independent reporting or analysis
- Sponsored content or thinly-veiled advertising
- Opinion pieces / hot takes that don't introduce new information
- Content that mentions the keyword in passing but is mainly about
  something unrelated
- Listicles / "top 10" roundups with no substantive analysis
- Reaction or reupload videos with no original reporting

## When it's ambiguous

- If a piece is borderline, lean toward collecting rather than skipping.
- The core question to ask: "Does this contain information a monitor would
  actually want to know about?"
